Enemies & Combat AI

Enemies are central to the game loop. The AI architecture allows combining different movement behaviors with special mechanics to create unique, challenging encounters. These are configured via EnemyDataSO assets and executed dynamically by EnemyController.

Enemy Movement Types

Defined by the EnemyMovementType enum, movement patterns are executed inside the Update() loop of EnemyController.cs:

Static & Patrol

  • Static: The enemy does not move. Useful for basic training dummies.
  • Patrol (Standard): Moves horizontally back and forth between coordinates. Flips directions if blocked by a wall.
  • Patrol (Ninja variant): wanders randomly within a 4-unit radius of its initial spawn position, clamping to arena bounds and recalculating paths if blocked.

Chase & Blink

  • ChasePlayer: Constantly moves towards the player position. Features a sliding algorithm: if directly blocked by a wall, it attempts to slide along the X-axis alone or the Y-axis alone.
  • Blink: Teleports to a random coordinates inside the arena at intervals based on the enemy's action cooldown. Triggers teleport dust VFX.

Special Combat Mechanics

Defined by the EnemySpecialMechanic enum, these add defensive layers or dangerous hazards:

1. FrontShield (Vanguard Shield)

Spawns a child object with a cyan overlay representing a front shield. The enemy slowly rotates to face the player (at 120°/second, allowing skilled players to launch behind them).

🛡️ Shield Block Formula

Upon collision with a dashing player, the script computes the dot product of the enemy's facing direction (right vector) and the player's relative position:

Vector2 relativePlayerPos = player.transform.position - transform.position;
float dot = Vector2.Dot(relativePlayerPos, transform.right);
if (dot > 0) {
    // Blocked!
}

If dot > 0, the player is in the front hemisphere. The shield blocks all damage, bounces the player backward with 50% velocity, triggers a sparks particle burst, plays the metal clash SFX, and shakes the camera.

2. ExplodeOnDeath (Bomber)

When HP falls to 0, instead of dying immediately, the enemy enters a fuse state (FusingRoutine):

  • Disables its colliders to prevent further damage.
  • Lerps (tweens) its scale up by +30%.
  • Flashes rapidly between its normal color and solid red (increasing frequency from 5Hz to 25Hz).
  • After a 1-second fuse, it detonates: shakes the screen, applies a massive knockback force to players within a 3-unit radius, and penalizes player life time by 10 seconds.

3. SplitOnDeath (Mitosis Slime)

Upon death, it instantiates two Mini Slimes at its position. The Mini Slimes have:

  • Only 1 HP and reward 10 points.
  • No special mechanics (they do not split again).
  • ChasePlayer movement type with a 1.5x speed boost.
  • Registers itself in the GameManager count to prevent premature floor clears.

4. WeaverDrone (Slow Zone Spawner)

Spawns purple, semi-translucent circular triggers called SlowZone at its position. The slow zones last for 4 seconds. Any player touching the trigger will have their velocity scaled down by 0.85x every frame (85% drag penalty, making aiming out of the zone extremely urgent).

Hit Registration Rules

  • When Player is Dashing: Enemy takes damage. Saturated damage is 1 (or 3 if Glass Blade is active) plus KineticMomentumBonusDamage multiplied by the number of wall bounces made during the current dash.
  • When Player is Idle/Aiming (Vulnerable): Player is hit. Penalizes life time by 10s (25s if Glass Blade is active), shakes camera, and applies a strong knockback force away from the enemy.
